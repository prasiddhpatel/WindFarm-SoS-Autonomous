# Dissertation Expansion Outline

## Target Length Strategy

A near-100-page report is realistic if the project is expanded into a structured technical dissertation rather than a short implementation summary. A workable page budget is:

1. Introduction and motivation: 6 pages.
2. Literature review: 14 pages.
3. Problem formulation and requirements: 8 pages.
4. System architecture: 10 pages.
5. UAV control and estimation: 10 pages.
6. Planning and perception: 10 pages.
7. Ground systems and crawler validation: 8 pages.
8. Fleet orchestration and digital twin: 10 pages.
9. Backend, dashboard, and deployment: 6 pages.
10. Simulation and testing: 8 pages.
11. Results and discussion: 8 pages.
12. Conclusion and future work: 4 pages.

This totals approximately 102 pages before appendices.

## Missing Material to Add

To fully justify that length, the final report should add:

- A deeper related-work chapter comparing aerial inspection, rotorcraft control near structures, ground docking systems, and blade NDE methods.
- A formal problem statement with assumptions, constraints, and optimization objectives.
- More mathematical detail for the controller, estimator, planner, and MRTA formulations.
- Dataset and model-training discussion for the perception stack.
- A full evaluation chapter using simulation and ablation studies.
- A limitations and ethics discussion about autonomy near critical energy assets.

## Appendix Candidates

Useful appendices include:

- Interface definitions and message schemas.
- Detailed database schema.
- Parameter tables.
- Simulation world configuration.
- Hardware bill of materials.
- Test matrix and CI evidence.
